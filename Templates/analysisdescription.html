Data Analysis



Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The process of analyzing data sets to summarize their main characteristics, often employing visual methods to uncover patterns, trends, and anomalies.

Descriptive Statistics

Techniques used to summarize and describe the key features of a data set, including measures such as mean, median, mode, variance, and standard deviation.

Inferential Statistics

Methods used to make inferences or predictions about a population based on a sample of data, including hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis.

Hypothesis Testing

Statistical tests used to assess the likelihood of observing a particular outcome under different conditions, helping to make decisions and draw conclusions about populations based on sample data.

Correlation Analysis

Examines the strength and direction of the relationship between two or more variables, often using correlation coefficients to quantify the degree of association.

Regression Analysis

Investigates the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables, estimating the effect of predictors on the outcome variable.

Time Series Analysis

Analyzes time-ordered data to identify patterns, trends, and seasonal variations, often using techniques such as decomposition, smoothing, and forecasting.

Clustering

Groups similar data points together based on their characteristics or attributes, aiming to uncover hidden patterns or structures within the data.

Classification

Assigns predefined categories or labels to data instances based on their features, often using machine learning algorithms to build predictive models.

Dimensionality Reduction

Reduces the number of variables or features in a data set while preserving its essential information, aiding in visualization, analysis, and model building.
